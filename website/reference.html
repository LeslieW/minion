References

MINION was started as an academic research project, and we envision that it will continue to be used in a research context, as well as being of use to the wider CP community.

The following paper describes the design principles behind Minion. It was chosen as one of the ten best papers out of about 500 submissions for ECAI 06.

    MINION: A Fast, Scalable, Constraint Solver
    Ian P. Gent, Chris Jefferson and Ian Miguel.
    To appear in The European Conference on Artificial Intelligence 2006 (ECAI 06).

Minion features "watched literals", an implementation idea for propagation algorithms which has proved very successful in SAT. The following paper describes watched literals and other kinds of watched triggers for constraint propagation.

    CPPod-17-2006: Watched Literals for Constraint Propagation in Minion
    Ian P. Gent, Chris Jefferson, Ian Miguel. CP Pod Report CPPod-17-2006, April 2006. (Slightly revised from a CP 2006 submission)
